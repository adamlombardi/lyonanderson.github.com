My Top iPhone Applications of 2010

Jan 3rd, 2010

I thought I’d share my favourite iPhone applications of 2010.

  • Byline - A great RSS reader that works with your Google Reader account. I’ve tried loads of different iPhone RSS readers, but this has the best offline support of any of them IMHO.

  • Tweetie 2 - Simply one of the best Twitter clients out there. I’d go further and say it’s one of the best iPhone application out there. Loren Brichter has made some great UI innovations my favourite being the pull down to refresh.

  • The Guardian - There are two feature I really like about the Guardians’s app. First, the tagging and cross-linking of articles. Second, the download and read offline feature. There’s some great content on here and the galleries have some stunning pictures. A very minor gripe is that does not remember where you are when you quit. As far as first versions go, this is the best I’ve seen for a long time.

  • Syncopy - This is a late addition - I’ve only been using it for a week or so, but I’ve been waiting for ages. Syncopy allows you to share your copy/cut/paste buffer across your Macs and iPhone. One of clinchers for me is that Syncopy keeps copies of your paste buffer items in the cloud.  You can be out and about and access your paste buffer history right on the iPhone. I know that’s probably not everyone’s cup of tea, but I find it quite useful - the paste buffer is an extension to my short term memory. A close second is Pastebot which in some ways is more functional (it handles images) and looks stunning. However, to sync both the iPhone and Mac apps have to be running at the same time.

  • Spotify - I cancelled my Napster subscription the instant this application came out. While it’s not one of the best iPhone applications yet, it is amazing to have access to all that music when you’re out and about. The offline capabilities are the real game changer though. It’s not perfect and I would like to see more control over playlists, more granular download/offline control and a homescreen like on the desktop version. I have to admit I do cheat a bit here and do use Backgrounder to allow me to play music while using other applications.

  • Evernote - I’ve been using Evernote since it was in beta and I love it! What really makes it for me is the OCR capabilities. I can scan or photograph anything and be able to search it at a later date. What distinguishes it from traditional OCR is that Evernote maintains a set of candidate words for each word in the image, thus improving the search chances. I now have over 600 notes which I can search and read wherever I am.  As with all these kinds of services it really comes down to how you use it. I now scan most letters, business cards and other random stuff. The beauty is that I just chuck it all in - I don’t need to file it.  As a bonus to premium customers, they just added downloading and offline search in the iPhone app.

  • reMail - Let’s you store your entire email account offline and search it.  It was great that Apple added search to built-in mail app, but it does not search the contents of emails. Being a child of Google I use my mailbox as a giant store of useful things and need to be able to search it. reMail let’s me do this and offline too. You can have multiple accounts (IMAP and GMail) and you can select which folders in those accounts are stored offline. The only downside is that you have to run the app periodically to let it download the mail - they do provide a notification services to help your remember.

A few other notable apps include DropBox, Facebook, Toodledo, Movies and iStat. If there was a theme to last year’s applications it would be to have access to, my ever growing, data in the cloud  wherever I am.
